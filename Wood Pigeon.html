Wood Pigeon (Rock Dove)

The UK's largest and commonest pigeon. The Wood Pigeon is generally grey with a pinkish breast and green, white and purple patch on the neck. The tail has a black tip and the wings have a prominent white patch. The eye is a bright yellow. The legs and bill are pink. Although shy in the countryside it can be tame and approachable in towns and cities. Its cooing call is a familiar sound in woodlands as is the loud clatter of its wings when it flies away. Wood Pigeons waddle when they walk, which adds to their general appearance of being overweight. In fact, the Wood Pigeon's feathers weigh more than its skeleton.

What they eat:

Seeds and grains, and green shoots when they're out in the fields: crops like cabbages, sprouts, peas and grain. Also buds, shoots, seeds, nuts and berries. They will feed on almost anything that is placed on a bird table. They also drink a lot, mainly because they do not get sufficient moisture from their food, unlike birds that eat earthworms, etc. An interesting feature about how they drink is that they use their beak like a straw, whereas other birds scoop the water up and throw their heads back to let it flow down their throats.

Natural habitats:

Woodland, Farmland, Grassland, Urban and suburban

Where to see them:

Woodpigeons are found across the UK in fields and woods, and also in towns and cities where they frequent parks and gardens.

When to see them:

Wood Pigeons can be seen all year round.
